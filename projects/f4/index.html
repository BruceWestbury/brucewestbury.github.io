The F₄ series

The F₄ series is a one-parameter family of diagrammatic categories associated to the sub-exceptional series of Lie algebras ending in F₄. The parameter \(n\) is the dimension of a representation carrying an invariant symmetric cubic tensor.

The fundamental relation is the six-term relation among four-boundary trivalent graphs. Together with reduction rules for bigons, triangles, and squares, this presentation allows evaluation of any closed trivalent graph of girth at least 5.

By computer search, every closed cubic graph of girth ≥ 5 up to 16 vertices can be evaluated using this relation. However, consistency of the evaluations requires the polynomial \[ (n-26)(n-14)(n-8)(n-5)(n-2)^2\, n\,(n+1)(n+2) = 0. \]

This is Thurston’s result (2004). The nine roots correspond to known Lie algebra representations in the F₄ row of Freudenthal’s magic square.
