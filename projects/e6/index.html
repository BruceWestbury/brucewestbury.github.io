The E₆ series

The E₆ series is a one-parameter family of diagrammatic categories associated to the sub-exceptional series of Lie algebras ending in E₆. The graphs involved are closed bipartite cubic graphs. The parameter \(n\) is the dimension of a representation carrying an invariant element of the third symmetric power.

The fundamental relation is the seven-term relation among five-boundary bipartite trivalent graphs. Together with reduction rules for bigons and squares, this allows evaluation of any closed bipartite cubic graph of girth at least 6.

Every closed bipartite cubic graph of girth ≥ 6 up to 22 vertices can be evaluated. Consistency requires the degree-12 polynomial \[ (n-27)(n-15)(n-9)(n-6)(n-3)^2(n-1)\,n^2(n+1)(n+3)^2 = 0 \] (Thurston 2004). The twelve roots correspond to Lie algebras in the E₆ row of Freudenthal’s magic square.

The computation at \(t = 22\) is larger than the F₄ computation: there are 406 source graphs yielding 406 relations.
